Featured case study

Plasma cell multi-omics workflow

An end-to-end single-cell pipeline investigating human plasma cell differentiation, integrating BD Rhapsody scRNA-seq, sample tag demultiplexing, stage-aware quality control, and a methodological comparison of cell cycle regression strategies. Re-analysis of Moreaux et al., Blood 2024.

Data & scale

BD Rhapsody scRNA-seq across four differentiation stages (MBC, prePB, PB, PC) in two biological replicates. 8,545 cells captured; 7,920 retained after stage-aware quality control.

Approach

Seurat v5 with LogNormalize, Harmony batch integration, and a three-way comparison of cell cycle regression strategies. Immunoglobulin genes excluded from variable features to reveal underlying regulatory programs.

Key finding

Unsupervised clustering recovered two PC sub-populations that segregate cleanly by immunoglobulin class (IgG-secreting and IgA/IgM-secreting plasma cells), without using Ig genes as clustering features.

Services

What I do

Single-cell transcriptomics

scRNA-seq and snRNA-seq analysis end-to-end: QC, integration, clustering, cell-type annotation, differential expression, trajectory analysis.

Single-cell epigenomics

scATAC-seq, snATAC-seq, and multiome workflows. Peak calling, motif analysis, peak-to-gene linkage, and chromatin-driven cell-state interpretation.

Gene regulatory networks

SCENIC+ and pycisTopic for enhancer-driven GRNs. hdWGCNA for co-expression modules. Integration of transcriptomic and epigenomic regulation.

Immune repertoire

BCR and TCR analysis using the Immcantation framework. Clonotype assembly, somatic hypermutation, isotype usage, repertoire diversity, transcriptome integration.

Non-coding RNA analysis

lncRNA regulatory networks integrating GENCODE annotation, co-expression, RIblast RNA-RNA interactions, and chromatin accessibility.

Reproducible pipelines

Modular, parameterized R and Python pipelines. Version-controlled, containerized when needed, and built for handoff to wet-lab collaborators.

About

Who I am

I'm Fabiola Blengio, a computational biologist with deep expertise in single-cell and multi-omics genomics, focused on the gene regulatory programs that shape immune cell identity. My current work integrates scRNA-seq, snATAC-seq, and lncRNA regulatory analysis to understand why long-lived plasma cells survive in some tissue niches but not others.

The name Lumen comes from the biology itself. A lumen is the space enclosed by coordinated, polarized cells, where organ function emerges. Function in biology is not stored in single cells; it emerges from how cells organize. That's what I help research teams see in their data.

I believe in reproducible work, clear communication, and the quiet conviction that it's never too late to become who you were meant to be.
